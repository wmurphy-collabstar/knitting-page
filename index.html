Needles

One important thing to keep in mind is the thickness of the needles. Thicker needles work well with thicker yarn, while thinner needles work for more delicate, thin yarn. They are measured by diameter in mm.

Another aspect is the kind of needle. There are a few types: straight needles, circle needles, and double pointed needles. They are all useful for different projects: circle needles for hats and socks, straight needles for scarves and blankets, etc. We list some common needle choices below.

//The way to choose needles is with this formula: idealNeedleThickness + needleType = idealNeedleForProject

  • 2.5mm circle needles
Yarn

An important factor to consider when it comes to yarn is the thickness of the yarn. Thicker yarn will help you make bushier sweaters and blackets, while with a thinner yarn you can make more intricate patterns for hats, etc.

Another important fact to consider is the type of yarn, the material itself. For colder months, wool would be a good material, while cotton is good for a lighter feel. We will list some popular yarns used for knitting projects.

//When picking a yarn to use for your project, the formula is yarnType + yarnThickness = idealYarnForProject
  • Thick wool
  • Thin cotton
Possible Projects
